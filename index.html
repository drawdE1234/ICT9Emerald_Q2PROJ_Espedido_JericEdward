Historical Spots of the Philippines

The Philippines is home to many places that revolve around the history and culture of people in its surroundings.


Here are The Top 3 Historical Sites of the Philippines

Rizal Park/Luneta Park

It is considered as the largest urban park of the Philippines and it is named after the country's national hero, Jose Rizal.


Source:
Rizal Park. (2024, January 25). In Wikipedia. https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rizal_Park

Cagsawa Ruins

These are the remnants of a 16th-Century church called The Cagsawa church along with the Cagsawa town after it was destroyed during the eruption of the Mayon Volcano.


Source:
Cagsawa Ruins. (2023, November 20). In Wikipedia. https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cagsawa_Ruins

Fort Santiago

It is a castle that Miguel Lopez de Legazpi, a Spanish explorer, constructed for the newly founded City of Manila.
